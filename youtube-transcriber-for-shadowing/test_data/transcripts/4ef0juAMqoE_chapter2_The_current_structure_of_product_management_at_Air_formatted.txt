It wasn't the people; it's the way they're working together. We no longer have the traditional product management function as it existed even a few years ago. We didn't get rid of people helping drive the product. What we did is we combined what one might call the inbound product development responsibilities of product managers with the outbound or marketing responsibilities of product marketing. That's the first thing we did. The second thing we did is we off-boarded much of the program management functions that product managers may do to actual program managers. A lot of people with the product management title are actually program managers. We actually started off-boarding some responsibilities to program managers.

The last thing we did is we made the group smaller and more senior. We don't really have a lot of junior product marketers. The most senior people are called product marketers, but everyone has to understand how to talk about the product. The basic idea is this: you can't build a product unless you know how to talk about the product. You can't be an expert in making the product unless you're also an expert in the market for it. A lot of companies, what they do is they ship a product, and it doesn't work. They say, "We tried that. It didn't work." If you say you tried it and it didn't work, my question is: was it a bad product, a bad strategy, or bad execution? Maybe it was a really well-made product, but you had no distribution plan. You had no way to talk about the product. If you build a great product and no one knows about it, did you even build a product?

That is essentially what we do. We have a much smaller function. The people are much more senior, and they have much more responsibility. The other thing, though, is that they do not control or drive design or engineering. We are a fairly purely functional model. They manage by influence; they do not have control. Now, you might ask, how does that work in a company where people can only manage by influence? Here's the amazing thing: we built and designed a company where you can manage by influence, and no one has to like you. You don't actually have to win people over.
