It was more of an asynchronous and ad hoc process. Once you hit 10, 15, or even 20 or 30 different mini pods shipping constantly, I think you need a bit more structure. High-risk decisions need to be surfaced effectively. 

We're iterating on this now. Any large rock that we have on the roadmap needs to be brought into the product review process, where both myself and the head of design are present to provide feedback. It’s important that this process is structured, specifically asking for the type of feedback needed and highlighting the key risks and trade-offs being made implicitly during that review. 

That’s one way we’re able to scale. However, I would say that largely, people ship without that level of scrutiny. The key difference between a beta and the general availability (GA) is where we really get plugged in. When we decide to go live to the rest of the customer base and ask sales to start selling, that’s when I come in to stress test the hypotheses and the decisions. 

This process occurs further downstream, which makes it more risky. But because we move so fast, we don’t waste much time if we have to pull something back.
