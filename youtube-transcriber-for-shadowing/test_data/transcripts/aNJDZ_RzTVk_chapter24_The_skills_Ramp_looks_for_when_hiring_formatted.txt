We look for people who have a very strong desire to have impact, and the best way to assess that is the impact that they've had or the reason why they are switching jobs. It goes back to what I was mentioning earlier in the chat, which was that velocity leads to people wanting to join because they want to have velocity. The best signal of that is when someone says, "I'm leaving because things got too slow, things got too bureaucratic." They often miss the old days where we were just building, shipping, and launching.

I look for people who can think deeply, so I'll go super deep into a decision or a trade-off that they had to make. I really scratch at that until I get to a deep understanding of how they make decisions and how deeply they think about things. In general, we tend to overemphasize those two skills rather than necessarily experience. Experience, again, to the point around ramp, is unique to each business and matters a lot less. You can have a lot less impact than your ability to be hungry and your ability to think deeply.
